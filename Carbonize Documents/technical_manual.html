Stage 1: Gas Conditioning & Venturi Quench Protocol

Stage 1 is the critical thermal shield and physical separator for the downstream components of the CMBSG. Because the biological engine in Stage 3 relies on immobilized, thermo-sensitive *CA-KR1* Carbonic Anhydrase enzymes, entering gases must be rapidly cooled and stripped of particulate contaminants.

1.1 Core Operational Targets

  • Thermal Shock Mitigation: Rapidly quench raw flue gas from $150\text{--}180^\circ\text{C}$ down to a saturated exit temperature of $40\text{--}45^\circ\text{C}$ in under $1.5\text{ seconds}$. This prevents heat from degrading the enzymes downstream.
  • Particle Stripping: Isolate and capture $>99.5\%$ of coarse fly ash ($\text{PM}_{10}$ and heavy soot particles). This blocks solids from blinding the fine kinetic meshes in Stage 3.
  • Agglomeration Bypass: Convert the trapped fly ash into a pumpable $40\text{ wt}\%$ solids bottom slurry. This slurry is routed around the enzyme tower directly to the Stage 4 pug-mill mixer to serve as a pozzolanic micro-aggregate.

1.2 Thermodynamic & Aerosol Governing Formulas

1.2.1 Gas Quench Energy Balance

The mass flow rate of cooling water required to achieve a saturated outlet temperature is modeled using a steady-state thermal energy balance:

$$\dot{Q}_{\text{quench}} = \dot{m}_{\text{gas}} \cdot \bar{C}_{p,\text{gas}} \cdot (T_{\text{in}} - T_{\text{out}}) + \dot{m}_{\text{ash}} \cdot C_{p,\text{ash}} \cdot (T_{\text{in}} - T_{\text{out}})$$ $$\dot{m}_{\text{evap}} = \frac{\dot{Q}_{\text{quench}}}{\Delta H_{\text{vap}}}$$
1.2.2 Particulate Collection Efficiency

The collection efficiency of fly ash particles into the water droplets is modeled via Calvert's venturi scrubbing relationship:

$$d_{50} = \sqrt{\frac{9 \cdot \mu_G \cdot d_d}{2 \pi \cdot v_t \cdot \rho_p \cdot C_C}}$$ $$C_C = 1 + \frac{2\lambda}{d_p} \left[ 1.257 + 0.400 \exp \left( -1.10 \frac{d_p}{2\lambda} \right) \right]$$

Raw chitosan requires protonating its primary amine groups ($-NH_2$) to convert into a cationic polyelectrolyte:

$$\text{R-NH}_2 + \text{H}^+ \rightleftharpoons \text{R-NH}_3^+$$ $$\alpha = \frac{1}{1 + 10^{\text{pH} - \text{pK}_a}}$$

3.1 Process Mass Transfer, Kinetics, & Catalysis Sizing

3.1.1 Carbonic Anhydrase (CA-KR1) Kinetic Hydration

The catalytic hydration velocity ($v$) is governed by Michaelis-Menten kinetics:

$$v = \frac{k_{\text{cat}} \cdot [E]_0 \cdot [\text{CO}_2]}{K_m + [\text{CO}_2]}$$
3.1.2 Two-Film Mass Transfer & Chemical Enhancement
$$N_{\text{CO}_2} = K_L \cdot E_{\text{en}} \cdot (C_{\text{CO}_2}^* - C_{\text{CO}_2,b})$$ $$Ha = \frac{\sqrt{D_{\text{CO}_2} \cdot k_{\text{enz}}}}{K_L}$$ $$E_{\text{en}} = \frac{Ha}{\tanh(Ha)}$$
3.1.3 Ultrasonic Demolding Acoustic Shear Stress

Peak acoustic shear stress ($\tau_{\text{shear}}$) generated at the wire-slurry interface must exceed the mineral gel's adhesion limit:

$$\tau_{\text{shear}} = 2\pi f \cdot \rho_{\text{solid}} \cdot c_{\text{sound}} \cdot A_{\text{amp}} \ge 2.2\text{ MPa}$$

HSE & Process Safety Protocol

The chemical loops inside the CMBSG utilize highly active reagents, strong organic acids, and concentrated heavy metal residues. Handling these reagents requires strict adherence to international safety parameters (OSHA / SPCB).

5.1 Chemical Hazard Assessment & Safe Handling

HF

Hydrofluoric Acid (HF) Etching Baths

Highly corrosive contact poison that causes systemic calcium decalcification. Primary mitigation includes a face velocity wet fume hood ($\ge 0.5\text{ m/s}$) and a pressurized 2.5 wt% Calcium Gluconate gel station within 3 meters.

HCN

Sodium Cyanoborohydride (HCN Risk)

Water-reactive reducing agent. If exposed to acidic conditions ($pH < 7.0$), it hydrolyzes to release lethal Hydrogen Cyanide ($HCN$) gas: $$\text{NaBH}_3\text{CN} + 3\text{H}_2\text{O} + \text{H}^+ \xrightarrow{\text{Acidic Media}} \text{HCN}\uparrow + \text{H}_3\text{BO}_3 + \text{Na}^+ + 3\text{H}_2\uparrow$$ Interlock hardware must cut the feed lines and flood the vessel with a 5 wt% sodium hypochlorite (NaOCl) oxidation solution if pH drops below 8.5.

5.2 Safety Instrumented Systems (SIS) & ESD Triggers

Trigger A: High pressure at Stage 3 base

If $PIT-102$ registers $P > 5,000\text{ Pa}$:

  • Executes in $\le 150\text{ ms}$
  • Opens pneumatic 3-way bypass gate V-1
  • De-energizes the ID fan instantly

Trigger B: Acidic pH drift in Reduction Bath

If $pH < 7.0$:

  • Executes in $\le 500\text{ ms}$
  • Trips the reagent feed pumps
  • Dumps NaOCl bleach to neutralize cyanides
