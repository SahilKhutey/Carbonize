## Phase 0 — Foundation (Month 0–2)

### Legal structure
- **Incorporate as a Private Limited Company** under the Companies Act, 2013 — this is the standard structure for startups that plan to raise equity from VCs/angels (LLPs and partnerships are DPIIT-eligible too, but Pvt Ltd is what nearly every institutional investor will expect for a priced or SAFE-like round).
- Get PAN, TAN, GST registration, and open a current account in the company's name.
- Budget ₹15,000–30,000 and 7–10 days for incorporation via a CA/CS or a platform (Vakilsearch, IndiaFilings, ClearTax, etc.).

### DPIIT Startup + Deep Tech recognition
As of the February 2026 DPIIT notification, this is materially more valuable than it used to be — do it early, it's free:
- Apply via the **National Single Window System (NSWS)** portal — the old Startup India portal no longer processes new recognition applications.
- **Apply for Deep Tech Startup recognition specifically**, not just standard Startup recognition. CarbonLattice's biocatalytic/biomineralization tech clearly fits: novel IP, high R&D intensity, long development cycles. Deep Tech status gets you a **20-year recognition window and ₹300 crore turnover cap**, versus 10 years / ₹200 crore for standard startups.
- The single biggest mistake founders make here is a vague innovation statement — this is the #1 rejection reason. Be specific: name the enzyme (carbonic anhydrase), the biopolymer (chitosan), the exact mechanism, and what makes it different from amine-based incumbents like Svante.
- **After recognition, immediately file for:**
  - **Section 80-IAC tax holiday** (separate application to the Inter-Ministerial Board, 45–90 days review) — 100% tax exemption on profits for any 3 consecutive years in your recognition window. Don't claim it in early loss-making years; save it for when you're actually profitable.
  - **80% patent filing fee rebate** and **50% trademark fee rebate**.
  - **SISFS (Startup India Seed Fund Scheme)** — grants up to ₹20 lakh for proof-of-concept and up to ₹50 lakh for prototype development/market trials. This is non-dilutive money worth pursuing in parallel with your pre-seed raise, not instead of it.
  - Note: angel tax under Section 56(2)(viib) was abolished from FY 2025-26, so that specific old pain point is gone — DPIIT recognition is now purely about the tax holiday, grants, and IP rebates, not angel-tax exemption.

### IP protection
- File a provisional patent on the core biomineralization/capture mechanism **before** you show the simulator or bench data to anyone outside a signed NDA. You already have a patent specification drafted (`pre_development/patent_p1_specification.md`) — get it in front of a patent attorney now, not after your first investor meeting.
- Use the 80% DPIIT patent-fee rebate once recognized.

### Co-founder
- This is your critical path item — most investors will ask about team composition before technology. Run this in parallel with everything else above, not after. (See the outreach playbook I built earlier — SINE IIT Bombay network, Climate Collective, CIIE.CO/NSRCEL alumni networks.)
- Target profile: business/GTM/fundraising ownership, ideally someone with industrial sales or carbon-market experience given CCTS is the tailwind.
- Lock equity split and vesting (standard 4-year vesting, 1-year cliff) in writing before you start fundraising together — investors will ask, and an undocumented handshake split is a red flag.

## Phase 1 — Validation (Month 1–4, overlaps Phase 0)

You already have simulation and techno-economic modeling done (CBMS-Sim). What's missing for a credible pre-seed pitch is physical proof and market proof:

### Technical validation
- Bench-scale trials demonstrating the biomineralization capture rate and block compressive strength against your simulator's predictions. Even a small-scale (lab bench, not pilot) result that validates your model against reality is worth more to an investor than another round of simulation refinement.
- Get this witnessed/documented by a university lab (IIT Bombay's new CCUS field lab is the obvious target — see outreach playbook) so it's third-party credible, not just your own lab notebook.

### Market validation
- Target 15–20 customer discovery conversations with mid-size industrial emitters (cement, steel, chemicals — the sectors covered by CCTS's ~490 facilities). You mentioned this is already underway; push for at least 3–5 **signed LOIs or pilot-interest letters**, even non-binding ones. Investors weight "a plant manager will let us test on-site" far higher than "we talked to 20 people."
- Map each conversation against CCTS's sector-specific intensity targets so your pitch can say precisely which regulatory clock each prospect is racing.

## Phase 2 — Pre-seed raise (Month 3–6)

### Target and sources
- **Raise target:** ₹50 lakh – ₹2 crore, blending non-dilutive (SISFS grant) and dilutive (angel/pre-seed) capital. Exact number depends on your bench-trial and pilot-build costs — build a 12–18 month runway budget before you set the ask.
- **Primary targets** (detailed in the outreach playbook): Speciale Invest (deep science/engineering focus), Climate Angels, Inflection Point Ventures, plus 1–2 angel checks sourced through SINE/Climate Collective warm intros.
- **Use of funds to state explicitly in the deck:** bench-to-pilot scale-up, first pilot deployment costs, 1–2 technical hires, working capital for the co-founder's GTM build.

### What the pitch needs by this point
- Bench validation data (Phase 1)
- 3–5 LOIs or pilot commitments
- A locked or near-locked co-founder
- DPIIT Deep Tech recognition (signals credibility + unlocks investor-visible tax benefits)
- A clean, professional data room (this is where the repo cleanup work matters — investors will ask for a data room or dig through your public GitHub)

## Phase 3 — Pilot build (Month 6–12)

- Deploy the first real pilot system at a partner industrial site — this is the single highest-value milestone on the entire roadmap. Everything before this is "promising science"; this is "it works in a real factory."
- Instrument the pilot heavily: capture efficiency, block strength, OPEX-per-tonne actuals vs. your simulator's predictions. The gap (or lack of gap) between simulated and real performance becomes your core seed-stage credibility argument.
- Begin patent prosecution in earnest (utility patent, not just provisional) once pilot data is in hand — real performance data strengthens claims.
- Start building the case study / reference customer story now; it's your best seed-stage asset.

---

## Phase 4 — Seed raise (Month 12–18)

- **Raise target:** ₹4–10 crore, informed by what multi-site rollout actually costs based on Phase 3 pilot economics.
- **Primary targets:** Theia Ventures (thesis-matched on IP-led advanced materials), NABARD's new climate-tech fund if the window is open, and by this stage you should also be eligible-adjacent for Third Derivative / Elemental Excelerator (they require 2+ FTE and TRL4 — a working pilot clears that bar).
- **What changes in the pitch:** move from "science + model" to "here's a working pilot, here's our unit economics, here's our CCTS-driven demand pipeline, here's our path to 10 sites."
- Consider **Ostara Advisors** or a similar climate-tech investment bank to run/support this round — sector-specific banks materially shorten the fundraising cycle for hardware-heavy climate deals.

## Key risks to watch

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Solo-founder team composition stalls a raise | Treat co-founder search as Phase 0 critical path, not a background task |
| Simulation-to-bench gap undermines credibility | Get third-party-witnessed bench validation before your first serious investor meeting |
| CCTS regulatory timeline slips, weakening urgency narrative | Track the Oct 2026 CCTS launch closely; have a value proposition that also works without regulatory tailwind (cost savings vs. incumbents) |
| Two parallel codebases (`packages/` vs `biomimetic_sim/`) read as unclear execution | Consolidate before showing the repo to any serious technical due-diligence investor |
| Running out of runway before pilot proof point | Blend non-dilutive SISFS/grant money with the pre-seed raise rather than relying on equity alone |
