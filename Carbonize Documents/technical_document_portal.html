Technical Reference & Standard Process Design

Continuous Multiphase Biomineralization and Solidification Grid

A multi-pollutant post-combustion capture and stabilization platform designed to operate at ambient temperature and near-atmospheric pressures. By combining enzymes and organic biopolymer templates, this system converts flue-gas volatiles ($CO_2$, $SO_x$, particulate dust, and heavy metals) directly into structural building blocks.

eco Coral-Inspired Synthesis

Mimics marine coral skeletal biomineralization. Uses the zinc-metalloprotein enzyme Carbonic Anhydrase ($CA$) to accelerate hydration velocity and a chitosan matrix to lower crystallization energy.

grid_view No Sequestration Storage Dependency

Eliminates the need for deep underground geological gas injection by capturing and converting gaseous waste into durable, non-leachable structural alternative-concrete blocks on-site.

Stage 1: Gas Conditioning & Venturi Quench Stack

Stage 1 is the thermal shield and primary particulate separation node. Because the biocatalytic engine downstream relies on thermo-sensitive, immobilized enzymes, raw flue gas exiting industrial ESP slipstreams must be rapidly cooled and stripped of abrasive soot.

1.1 Sizing Calculations & Heat Balances

1.1.1 Steady-State Quench Energy Balance

The cooling water demand required to reach a saturated outlet temperature is modeled using a steady-state thermal energy balance:

$$\dot{Q}_{\text{quench}} = \dot{m}_{\text{gas}} \cdot \bar{C}_{p,\text{gas}} \cdot (T_{\text{in}} - T_{\text{out}}) + \dot{m}_{\text{ash}} \cdot C_{p,\text{ash}} \cdot (T_{\text{in}} - T_{\text{out}})$$ $$\dot{m}_{\text{evap}} = \frac{\dot{Q}_{\text{quench}}}{\Delta H_{\text{vap}}}$$
1.1.2 Particulate Collection Efficiency

Using Calvert's venturi scrubbing model, the cut diameter ($d_{50}$) collected at $50\%$ efficiency matches:

$$d_{50} = \sqrt{\frac{9 \cdot \mu_G \cdot d_d}{2 \pi \cdot v_t \cdot \rho_p \cdot C_C}}$$
1.1.3 Scrubber Throat Pressure Drop

The total auxiliary pressure drop ($\Delta P_{\text{venturi}}$) added by the high-velocity throat zone is modeled via:

$$\Delta P_{\text{venturi}} = 5 \times 10^{-5} \cdot v_t^2 \cdot \rho_G \cdot \left( \frac{Q_L}{Q_G} \right)$$

Stage 2: Ionic Leachate & Matrix Blending Loop

Stage 2 prepares and blends the semi-organic carrier fluid. It must maintain a precise ratio of dissolved divalent calcium ions ($Ca^{2+}$), protonated chitosan chains, and active catalysts under a strictly controlled pH envelope to prevent premature mineral precipitation inside feedlines.

2.1 Chemical Thermodynamics & Dissolution Kinetics

2.1.1 Slag Leaching (Shrinking Core Model)

Dissolution rate of crushed basic oxygen furnace slag is diffusion-limited and calculated via:

$$\text{CaO} + 2\text{H}^+ \rightarrow \text{Ca}^{2+} + \text{H}_2\text{O}$$ $$1 - 3(1-X)^{2/3} + 2(1-X) = k_{\text{diff}} \cdot t$$
2.1.2 Chitosan Protonation Equilibrium

To solubilize marine-derived chitosan flakes, primary amine groups must be protonated using weak organic acids:

$$\text{R-NH}_2 + \text{H}^+ \rightleftharpoons \text{R-NH}_3^+$$ $$\alpha = \frac{1}{1 + 10^{\text{pH} - \text{pK}_a}}$$
2.1.3 Calcite Saturation Index (SI)

To suppress premature calcite precipitation before gas exposure, the Saturation Index must remain strictly negative ($SI < 0$):

$$SI = \log_{10} \left( \frac{[\text{Ca}^{2+}][\text{CO}_3^{2-}]}{K_{\text{sp}}} \right) < 0$$

2.2 Mechanical Sizing & Vessel Specifications

  • Leaching Reactor (R-201): Jacketed, glass-lined vessel with a capacity of $15,000\text{ Liters}$ ($\varnothing 2,400\text{ mm}$ internal diameter).
  • Dissolution Vessel (T-202): Mirror-polished 316L Stainless Steel tank ($12,000\text{ Liters}$ working volume). Equipped with vertical wall baffles to prevent polymer gel rotational swirling.
  • Helical Static Mixer (MX-203): Inline Schedule 80 CPVC mixer ($1,800\text{ mm}$ length, diameter $\varnothing 150\text{ mm}$).

Stage 3: Kinetic Mesh Absorber Column

Stage 3 is the reaction core where gaseous carbon dioxide and sulfur compounds contact the trickling biopolymer matrix film across multiple gradient static wire screens.

3.1 Mass Transfer, Catalysis & Acoustic Wave Mechanics

3.1.1 Carbonic Anhydrase (CA-KR1) Catalytic Hydration

Hydration of dissolved carbon dioxide is catalyzed by immobilized, thermostable *CA-KR1* enzymes via Michaelis-Menten kinetics:

$$v = \frac{k_{\text{cat}} \cdot [E]_0 \cdot [\text{CO}_2]}{K_m + [\text{CO}_2]}$$
3.1.2 Chemical Enhancement Factor (Hatta Number)

Chemical absorption kinetics through the pseudoplastic matrix film matches Two-Film Theory with chemical enhancement:

$$N_{\text{CO}_2} = K_L \cdot E_{\text{en}} \cdot (C_{\text{CO}_2}^* - C_{\text{CO}_2,b})$$ $$Ha = \frac{\sqrt{D_{\text{CO}_2} \cdot k_{\text{enz}}}}{K_L}$$
3.1.3 Ultrasonic Demolding Acoustic Shear Stress

Peak acoustic shear stress ($\tau_{\text{shear}}$) generated at the wire-slurry interface must exceed the mineral gel's adhesion limit:

$$\tau_{\text{shear}} = 2\pi f \cdot \rho_{\text{solid}} \cdot c_{\text{sound}} \cdot A_{\text{amp}} \ge 2.2\text{ MPa}$$

3.2 Gradient Mesh Array Geometry

  • Tower Shell: Rectangular structural column duct measuring $2.5 \times 2.5\text{ m}$ base with $12.0\text{ m}$ total column height.
  • Woven Screen Configurations:
      • Layer 1 (Coarse Top): 20 mesh ($\varnothing 0.40\text{ mm}$ wires) optimized for heavy particulate intercepts.
      • Layer 2 (Intermediate): 80 mesh ($\varnothing 0.13\text{ mm}$ wires) optimized for desulfurization and heavy metals.
      • Layer 3 (Fine Bottom): 150 mesh ($\varnothing 0.06\text{ mm}$ wires) optimized for enzymatic carbon mineralization.

Stage 4: Consolidation & Press Foundry Protocol

Stage 4 acts as the physical crystallization, productization, and extraction module of the CMBSG. Its structural objective is to continuously co-blend, de-water, compact, and extrude the carbonated, metal-rich biopolymer aragonite gel from Stage 3 and the coarse fly ash bottom slurry from Stage 1 into high-strength, non-leaching structural eco-blocks.

4.1 Process Kinetics & Compressive Strength Models

4.1.1 Pozzolanic Hydration Kinetics

The coarse boiler fly ash rich in amorphous silica and alumina reacts with dissolved basic calcium hydroxides to initiate slowly growing C-S-H cementitious gels:

$$\text{Ca(OH)}_2 + \text{SiO}_2 + \text{H}_2\text{O} \rightarrow \text{C-S-H (Calcium Silicate Hydrate Gel)}$$ $$3\text{Ca}^{2+} + 2\text{SiO}_3^{2-} + 3\text{H}_2\text{O} \rightarrow 3\text{CaO} \cdot 2\text{SiO}_2 \cdot 3\text{H}_2\text{O}\downarrow$$
4.1.2 Biopolymer Amine Condensation & Cross-linking

Mechanical force (up to 200 bar) drives out bound water, deprotonating active primary amines to coordinate around nanocrystals:

$$\text{R-NH}_3^+ + \text{OH}^- \rightleftharpoons \text{R-NH}_2 + \text{H}_2\text{O}$$
4.1.3 Empirical Compressive Strength Matrix

Ultimate 48-hour structural compressive strength ($\sigma_c$) in MPa scales with aggregate-to-matrix interlocking boundaries:

$$\sigma_c = \alpha \cdot \left(\frac{m_{\text{ash}}}{m_{\text{CaCO}_3}}\right)^\beta \cdot \ln(P_{\text{comp}})$$

HSE & Process Safety Protocol

Industrial safety protocol mapping chemical, acoustic, and high-pressure mechanical hazards across the Multi-Pollutant Solidification Grid.

⚠️ Critical Chemical Risk A: Hydrofluoric Acid (HF) Bath

Used in the Stage 3 wire mesh etching prep. HF is a systemic dermal toxin that penetrates skin rapidly to chelate blood calcium, causing severe cardiac risk.

Required Mitigation: Store and maintain high-pressure Calcium Gluconate (2.5 wt%) gel antidote stations within 3 meters of the reaction bath.

⚠️ Critical Chemical Risk B: Sodium Cyanoborohydride (HCN Risk)

Used to reduce imine Schiff bases during enzyme coupling. Contact with acids ($pH < 7.0$) hydrolyzes cyanoborohydride instantly to release lethal volumes of Hydrogen Cyanide gas:

$$\text{NaBH}_3\text{CN} + 3\text{H}_2\text{O} + \text{H}^+ \xrightarrow{\text{Acidic Media}} \text{HCN}\uparrow + \text{H}_3\text{BO}_3 + \text{Na}^+ + 3\text{H}_2\uparrow$$

Hardwired Rule: Reaction storage tanks must be locked above pH 8.5 via automated sodium hypochlorite flood valves.
