# CBMS-Sim Laboratory Partnership Strategy

## 1. Executive Summary
To validate the coupled multi-species capture model and reduce parameter uncertainties by \(\ge 40\%\), CBMS-Sim requires access to high-fidelity analytical instrumentation and pilot scaling environments. This document outlines the hybrid partnership strategy combining academic research groups for complex reaction kinetics/precipitation and commercial Contract Research Organizations (CROs) for standard high-throughput assays.

---

## 2. Decision Framework

*   **Academic Partnerships (Subsidized Rates)**: Best for highly specialized physical chemistry, bioprocess engineering, and custom JIT assays where expertise overrides time-to-market.
*   **Commercial CROs (Market Rates)**: Best for standard, highly regulated assays (e.g. heavy metal extraction, EPA-aligned testing) requiring certified Quality Control and fast turnaround.
*   **In-house Facility (CAPEX)**: Deferred to Year 2 for pilot validation rigs (CE-4).

## 4. MoU & IP Allocation Framework

To protect CBMS Technologies Pvt. Ltd.'s core trade secrets and competitive advantage, all MoUs with academic partners must adhere to the following IP rules:

1.  **Background IP**: Any pre-existing IP of both parties remains exclusively owned by the respective party.
2.  **Foreground IP**: CBMS retains ownership of all project-derived intellectual property, patents, and software files. Academic PIs are granted licensing rights for non-commercial research use only.
3.  **Publication Rights**: Academic partners have the right to publish scientific results subject to a 90-day review period by CBMS to strip proprietary parameters and file patents.
4.  **Raw Data**: All raw experimental outputs, mass spectrometer files, and logs belong to CBMS.
