# Patent Specification Draft (P-1)

## 1. Title of the Invention
**A STABLE BIOPOLYMER MATRIX COMPOSITION FOR SIMULTANEOUS MULTI-POLLUTANT EMISSION CONTROL AND INSITU CARBON MINERALIZATION**

## 2. Field of the Invention
This invention relates to the field of chemical engineering, industrial pollution control, and biomineralization-based carbon capture. Specifically, it relates to a stable, reusable biopolymer-enzyme composition designed to simultaneously absorb and solidify carbon dioxide ($\text{CO}_2$), sulfur dioxide ($\text{SO}_2$), particulates, and toxic heavy metals from flue gas streams into construction-grade solid matrices.

## 3. Background & Prior Art
Conventional post-combustion carbon capture systems rely on amine solvents (e.g., Monoethanolamine) that suffer from high thermal regeneration penalties. Abiotic mineral carbonation requires elevated pressures and temperatures to leach metal ions and precipitate carbonates. 

While enzyme-catalyzed mineralization using Carbonic Anhydrase (CA) has been demonstrated in liquid buffers, the enzyme rapidly deactivates under the high temperature, acidity, and sulfur concentrations typical of raw flue gas. Furthermore, there is no existing single-vessel composition that combines carbon capture, desulfurization, particulate trapping, and heavy metal chelation without generating secondary liquid hazardous wastes.

## 4. Summary of the Invention
The present invention overcomes the limitations of the prior art by providing a stable, cross-linked biopolymer matrix composition containing:
1.  **A Cationic Chitosan Template:** Sourced from deacetylated chitin biowaste, protonated to present positive surface charges ($\text{R-NH}_3^+$) that bind divalent calcium ($\text{Ca}^{2+}$) ions and chelate toxic transition metals.
2.  **A Recombinant Thermo-Alkaliphilic Carbonic Anhydrase Enzyme:** Structurally stabilized and immobilized within the chitosan gel network, remaining active at temperatures up to $50^\circ\text{C}$ and pH ranges of 8.0–9.5.
3.  **An Organic Cross-Linker:** Selected from citric acid, glutaraldehyde, or genipin, forming a covalent hydrogel network that prevents biopolymer depolymerization under continuous acid gas exposure.

## 5. Detailed Description
The biopolymer matrix comprises an aqueous slurry of:
*   **Chitosan:** $1.5\text{--}3.5\text{ wt}\%$, with a molecular weight of 150–300 kDa and a degree of deacetylation $>85\%$.
*   **Calcium Source:** $2.0\text{--}5.0\text{ wt}\%$ calcium ions ($\text{Ca}^{2+}$) obtained from recycled industrial waste (steel slag or kiln dust leachate).
*   **Carbonic Anhydrase:** $5\text{--}20\text{ mg/L}$ of recombinant variant *CA-KR1*.
*   **Cross-linking Agent:** $0.05\text{--}0.2\text{ wt}\%$ citric acid.

When flue gas bubbles through the trickling gel matrix, dissolved carbon dioxide is hydrologically converted to bicarbonate by the CA enzyme. The localized high concentration of calcium ions bound to the chitosan templates triggers immediate nucleation of calcium carbonate calcite and aragonite crystals. Concurrently, free primary amine groups ($\text{R-NH}_2$) coordinate transition metal cations (such as mercury, lead, and cadmium) into stable, non-leaching complexes.

## 6. Claims
We claim:
1.  A biopolymer matrix composition for industrial flue gas treatment, comprising:
    *   an aqueous carrier gel containing $1.5\text{ to } 3.5\text{ wt}\%$ of deacetylated chitosan;
    *   $2.0\text{ to } 5.0\text{ wt}\%$ of dissolved calcium ions ($\text{Ca}^{2+}$);
    *   $5.0\text{ to } 20.0\text{ mg/L}$ of Carbonic Anhydrase enzyme; and
    *   $0.05\text{ to } 0.2\text{ wt}\%$ of a cross-linking agent.
2.  The composition as claimed in claim 1, wherein the chitosan has a degree of deacetylation greater than $85\%$ and a molecular weight of 150 to 300 kDa.
3.  The composition as claimed in claim 1, wherein the Carbonic Anhydrase is a thermo-alkaliphilic variant *CA-KR1*.
4.  The composition as claimed in claim 1, wherein the cross-linking agent is citric acid.
5.  The composition as claimed in claim 1, wherein the calcium ions are derived from basic oxygen furnace steel slag or cement kiln dust.
6.  The composition as claimed in claim 1, wherein the biopolymer matrix simultaneously sequesters carbon dioxide and desulfurizes flue gas by co-precipitating calcium carbonate and calcium sulfate within the chitosan hydrogel network.
