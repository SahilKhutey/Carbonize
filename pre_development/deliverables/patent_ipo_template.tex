%--- DESCRIPTION ---
\section*{FIELD OF THE INVENTION}
The present invention relates generally to industrial emissions control and post-combustion carbon capture. More particularly, the invention relates to a stabilized, cross-linked biopolymer matrix composition containing immobilized carbonic anhydrase enzymes that simultaneously sequesters carbon dioxide, sulfur dioxide, fine particulates, and heavy metals directly from hot raw flue gas streams into solid alternative-concrete blocks.

\section*{BACKGROUND OF THE INVENTION}
Industrial gas scrubbers traditionally address single pollutants in isolation. Conventional carbon capture relies on volatile organic amine solvents requiring high-temperature steam regeneration. Abiotic mineral carbonation projects suffer from slow reaction kinetics and high energy penalties associated with high-pressure grinding and carbonation systems. 

A major challenge in biological carbonation is that carbonic anhydrase enzymes undergo rapid thermal and acidic deactivation under typical flue gas conditions. There is a critical need for a stable, single-vessel matrix composition that coordinates these separate reaction pathways without liquid waste streams.

\section*{SUMMARY OF THE INVENTION}
The present invention resolves these challenges by providing a stable biopolymer matrix composition. The composition includes a deacetylated chitosan polyelectrolyte template, a divalent calcium source, an immobilized thermo-alkaliphilic carbonic anhydrase variant, and an organic cross-linking agent.

\section*{DETAILED DESCRIPTION OF THE INVENTION}
In a preferred embodiment, the composition is an aqueous slurry comprising:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item $1.5$ to $3.5$ wt\% of chitosan biopolymer having a molecular weight of 150 to 300 kDa;
    \item $2.0$ to $5.0$ wt\% of divalent calcium ions ($\text{Ca}^{2+}$) obtained from basic oxygen furnace slag;
    \item $5.0$ to $20.0$ mg/L of a thermo-alkaliphilic carbonic anhydrase enzyme variant *CA-KR1*; and
    \item $0.05$ to $0.2$ wt\% of a citric acid cross-linker.
\end{enumerate}

The cationic chitosan template stabilizes the enzyme and decreases the calcite nucleation energy barrier, while primary amine groups coordinate volatile heavy metals such as lead and mercury.

\section*{WE CLAIM:}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item A biopolymer matrix composition for industrial flue gas sequestration, comprising:
    \begin{itemize}
        \item an aqueous carrier gel containing $1.5$ to $3.5$ wt\% of deacetylated chitosan;
        \item $2.0$ to $5.0$ wt\% of dissolved calcium ions ($\text{Ca}^{2+}$);
        \item $5.0$ to $20.0$ mg/L of Carbonic Anhydrase enzyme; and
        \item $0.05$ to $0.2$ wt\% of a cross-linking agent.
    \end{itemize}
    
    \item The composition as claimed in claim 1, wherein the chitosan has a degree of deacetylation greater than $85\%$ and a molecular weight of 150 to 300 kDa.
    
    \item The composition as claimed in claim 1, wherein the Carbonic Anhydrase is a thermo-alkaliphilic variant *CA-KR1*.
    
    \item The composition as claimed in claim 1, wherein the cross-linking agent is citric acid.
    
    \item The composition as claimed in claim 1, wherein the calcium ions are derived from basic oxygen furnace steel slag leachate.
    
    \item The composition as claimed in claim 1, wherein the biopolymer matrix simultaneously sequesters carbon dioxide and desulfurizes flue gas by co-precipitating calcium carbonate and calcium sulfate within the chitosan hydrogel network.
\end{enumerate}

%--- ABSTRACT ---
\section*{ABSTRACT}
The present invention discloses a stable biopolymer matrix composition for simultaneous multi-pollutant emission control and in-situ carbon mineralization. The composition comprises an aqueous gel of deacetylated chitosan ($1.5$--$3.5$ wt\%), dissolved calcium ions ($2.0$--$5.0$ wt\%), a recombinant thermo-alkaliphilic carbonic anhydrase enzyme ($5.0$--$20.0$ mg/L), and an organic cross-linking agent ($0.05$--$0.2$ wt\%). The composition simultaneously captures carbon dioxide ($\text{CO}_2$), sulfur dioxide ($\text{SO}_2$), particulates, and toxic heavy metals directly from raw flue gas streams, solidifying the captured pollutants into structural composite building blocks.
