# Memorandums of Understanding (MoU) Frameworks

This document contains templates and structural outlines for collaboration MoUs with academic partners for the CBMS project.

---

## 1. MoU Framework: IIT Bombay (CCU CoE)
*Target Division: National Centre of Excellence in Carbon Capture and Utilization (NCoE-CC), IIT Bombay*

### 1.1 Purpose of Collaboration
To leverage IIT Bombay’s leadership in carbon capture and storage technologies, accessing their pilot-scale infrastructure, testing laboratories, and engineering consulting networks to validate the mechanical and industrial feasibility of the CBMS.

### 1.2 Resource Allocation & Laboratory Access
*   **Infrastructure Access:** CBMS researchers will be granted access to the analytical facilities at IIT Bombay (including GPC, ICP-MS, XRD, and environmental testing laboratories).
*   **Shared Space:** Allocation of a dedicated bench space in the Chemical Engineering laboratory for the assembly of the 10L bubble column prototype.
*   **Academic Supervision:** Co-opt a faculty advisor from the CCU CoE to act as a scientific steering member.

### 1.3 Intellectual Property & Commercialization
*   **Background IP:** Background IP created by either party prior to this MoU remains the sole property of that party (protecting CBMS’s core biopolymer configuration claims).
*   **Foreground IP:** Intellectual property generated during joint research under this collaboration will be co-owned. CBMS retains the exclusive, global, royalty-free commercialization rights, while IIT Bombay retains non-commercial academic publication rights.

## 2. MoU Framework: IISc Bangalore
*Target Division: Department of Inorganic and Physical Chemistry & Department of Materials Engineering, Indian Institute of Science (IISc)*

### 2.1 Purpose of Collaboration
To utilize IISc’s scientific resources in structural polymer synthesis and enzyme kinetics to validate the molecular stability of the chitosan matrix and the activity of the *CA-KR1* enzyme in high-viscosity hydrogels.

### 2.2 Resource Allocation & Laboratory Access
*   **Analytical Access:** Access to high-resolution NMR spectrometers, stopped-flow kinetics spectrophotometers, and Rheological characterization facilities.
*   **Biotech Synthesis:** Collaboration with the biochemistry divisions to optimize the recombinant E. coli fermentation strains for bulk production of the *CA-KR1* enzyme.

### 2.3 Intellectual Property & Publication
*   **IP Rights:** Any modifications to the enzyme sequence or biopolymer cross-linking methods developed at IISc will be co-patented. CBMS retains a first right of refusal for exclusive commercial licensing.
*   **Publication:** Joint co-authorship of peer-reviewed papers (Targeting *Environmental Science & Technology* and *Chemical Engineering Journal*).
